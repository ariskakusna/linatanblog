Meet the Experts

  • Sanjay Batra, PhD, is the co-founder of WeThrivv and founder of VIAS Partners in Doylestown, Pennsylvania.

What are some of the benefits to using scalp massagers?

A good scalp massage can not only bring down the stress from a jammed day, the practice can also play a big role in improving your overall hair health. Essentially, the best scalp massagers help with circulation and can alter the amount of oxygen pumping in and out of your hair follicles.

“Scientists have discovered oxygen may be limited in scalps with thinning hair or hair loss,” Dr. Batra explains. Without the proper airflow, he says, certain hormones that need to pass through the scalp can build up in the follicle, adding stress and possibly even causing the follicle to die.

Who shouldn’t use a scalp massager?

Batra notes that you shouldn’t use a scalp massager if you have any active inflammation from, say, seborrheic dermatitis or any other irritating scalp conditions. He advises waiting until the inflammation lessens, and double-checking with your dermatologist before adding a scalp massager to your regimen.
